the core claim

ethics

Animals are sentient beings with their own experiences. They are not objects whose value depends on how useful they are to humans.

The ethical question is not whether animals are identical to humans. It is whether their ability to suffer, fear, bond, and want to live should matter morally.

questions to ask

reflection
  1. If intelligence determined moral worth, would less intelligent humans deserve less protection?
  2. If pleasure justified harm, would taste pleasure justify harming a companion animal?
  3. If culture justified violence, would any tradition be beyond criticism?
